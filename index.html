The Basics

Call Notes
You are not calling to sell a policy. You are calling to help the client face a responsibility they already showed interest in handling. The lead already raised their hand. Your job is to calmly help them uncover it, explain it, and take ownership of it.
Client Should Think"This agent is not pushing me. He is helping me think clearly about something I have been avoiding."
Silence Is Not Rejection
When you ask a powerful question, stop talking. Do not rescue them from the silence. Do not soften the question. Do not answer for them. Let the client sit with their own answer.
The Four Connections
Connect These1. Who they love.  2. What happens with no plan.  3. The emotional & financial cost.  4. What affordable action they can take today.
How You Should Sound
Calm. Grounded. Warm. Certain. Not hype. Slow. Soft but firm. No rushing. No nervous laughter. No talking over the client.
Pause Timing Rules
Use Throughout the Script
⏸ [1–2 SEC] — After normal questions. Keeps you from sounding rushed.

⏸ [3–5 SEC] — After emotional or problem-awareness questions.

⏸ [5–7 SEC] — After heavy questions about burden, death, family, money, grief.

⏸ [8–12 SEC] — After presenting options. The buying silence. First to speak gives up control.

⏸ [UP TO 20 SEC] — When client is clearly processing. If still silent after 20 sec, softly say: "Take your time. I know this is something you want to think through correctly." Then stay quiet again.
Before Each Call, Say
"I am calm. I am here to serve. I am not chasing. I am guiding. I will ask good questions. I will let the client think. I will not rescue them from silence."

📋 Sale Summary
You need to develop the mind of a trusted advisor, not just a salesperson.
The DifferenceA salesperson thinks: "How do I get them to buy?" A trusted advisor thinks: "How do I help this person make the decision they already know they need to make?"
Weak vs. Strong
A weak agent chases the sale. A strong agent controls the frame.
A weak agent over-explains. A strong agent asks better questions.
A weak agent fears silence. A strong agent uses silence.
A weak agent gets uncomfortable when the client feels emotion. A strong agent understands that emotion is where the truth comes out.
Remember
The TruthOne day, the person on the phone will not be here. One day, the beneficiary may be standing in a funeral home, trying to figure out how to pay. One day, the paperwork you helped create may be the difference between panic and peace.
The Final Rule
Commit This to Memory
Never rush the emotional parts.
Never rush the budget.
Never rush the close.

Ask the question. Pause. Let them answer. Then guide them.
